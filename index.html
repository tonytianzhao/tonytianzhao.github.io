Tian at a glance

I was born and grew up in Yangzhou, a 2,501-year-old Chinese city where the Grand Canal meets Yangtze River. In 2013 I was enrolled in Nanjing University, working towards a B.S. in Geographical Sciences. Meanwhile, I have been serving as the Class Monitor to speak for my class since September 2014.

It is Professor Shu GAO who led me into the fascinating research of coastal oceanography. Currently I am working with Prof. Shu GAO, Prof. Yaping WANG and Dr. Qian YU on sediment dynamics. I am particularly interested in coastal/nearshore sediment transport and morphodynamics, both in the normal scenario and during extreme events (eg. storm surges). My future goal is to promote a deeper understanding of sediment dynamic processes by combining observation (in-situ & remote) and modeling (established & original) approaches together, and to convey the proceedings to both academia and the public.

Other than science, I also enjoy taking railway journeys, hiking along highways, reading the classics, speaking Chinese dialects (Lower Yangtze, Wu and Minnan), singing traditional folk songs, and being a gourmet.
